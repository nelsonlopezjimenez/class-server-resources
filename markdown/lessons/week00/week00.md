Navigating This Course
Navigating This Course
What is a Module?
The best way to navigate this course is through the Course Modules. This method makes it easy for you to be sure you have completed each item during the week by completing the entire "Module."

How do Modules work?
Here is how you will complete the course assignments each week. The course is divided into weekly modules with text headers such as READ, WATCH, EXPLORE, COMPLETE, DISCUSS, and REVIEW. All of your readings, assignments, videos, and tasks for the week are in each weekly module. There are no hidden assignments or surprise assignments.

Your job will be to go to each of the sections in the module and explore the material provided. When you are viewing any of the sections, you will click each link (the words in blue), or the pictures (if there are videos) and read/watch/explore/complete the material you will find there. As we move through the quarter, you may prefer to minimize modules to keep from having to scroll so much. It's up to you!

Accommodations for Students
Even though the course is accessible for all students, there may also be a need for some students to seek additional accommodations. Students needing disability assistance are encouraged to contact the Student Services for Disabilities Office and/or the Dean of Corrections. Services for Students with Disabilities (SSD) ensures that programs at Edmonds College are accessible and usable by all students, regardless of ability. If you require an accommodation for a disability, or if you have struggled with learning in the past and might benefit from a consultation, please contact SSD online or by phone: 425.640.1320 or email: ssdmail@edmonds.edu, or contact the Dean of Corrections Education.

Accessibility Features in This Course
The materials in the modules have been carefully tailored to meet the needs of all students, including students with disabilities. This class has undergone a rigorous accessibility analysis and uses universal design learning (Links to an external site.) strategies to help all students have access to the material in the class. When possible, course materials and resources available in a digital format on Canvas are able to be downloaded in accessible formats; however, if you do encounter a barrier, please contact your instructor as soon as possible to address the issue.

Canvas has features, such as Ally, that help instructors create a more accessible course for all students. Below is a list of features that allow students to utilize this course in different formats.

Pages and files that have a drop down feature with an A icon (located at the top of the page) will allow the page or document to be read in other formats, including:

epub for e-reading
electronic braille readers
MP3 audio files
Bee-line readers for faster on-screen reading.
